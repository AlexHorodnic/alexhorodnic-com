Product Context

One connected buying journey

Ecommerce interfaces often look polished in isolation but lose credibility when product choices, cart contents, filters, or checkout progress stop behaving as one system.

I built the complete frontend journey around 40 typed demo products across 10 categories, from discovery and product comparison through cart management and order confirmation.

Engineering Approach

State follows the customer

Feature routes stay lazy-loaded while shared services preserve useful choices across the browsing and checkout flow.

01

Derived commerce state

Signals and computed values keep cart quantities, subtotal, discounts, shipping, tax, and final totals synchronized.

02

Guarded persistence

Runtime guards validate stored cart, filter, variant, and shipping data before it re-enters application state.

03

Feature boundaries

Catalog, product detail, cart, and checkout own their behavior while reusable product and feedback components stay shared.

Key Challenge

Continuity without careless persistence

A useful checkout should remember progress, but a frontend demo should not store payment details simply to feel realistic.

The decision

The four-step flow gates later stages behind valid shipping and payment fields. Shipping details are restored after refresh through a debounced, validated snapshot.

Card fields are deliberately excluded from persistence. On mobile, progress condenses and the primary action stays reachable in a sticky bottom bar.

Tradeoffs

Frontend depth, honest boundaries

Vertex is a frontend portfolio project. Products are typed mock data, images come from Unsplash, and checkout never processes or stores a real payment.

A production storefront would require inventory and pricing APIs, authentication, server-owned carts, payment-provider tokenization, analytics, observability, and broader automated test coverage.
